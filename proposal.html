Project Proposal: Course Evaluation Website

Problem/Data Domain

Topic Overview

Course selection is a crucial aspect of every UW student's academic journey, yet it often becomes a source of frustration and headaches. The large student population in a public institute, coupled with popular courses filling up quickly, creates a competitive environment where students must wake up early and navigate a frantic registration process. Hence it is always necessary to plan ahead. The current official platforms like MyPlan or DawgPath offer only a cursory glimpse into course content, which makes it exceedingly difficult to make informed decisions. As a result, UW students might find themselves enrolled in classes that are either too challenging or do not align with their academic plans, posing risks to their GPA and time management; and by then it is often too late to switch to another class. We need tools to efficiently provide detailed course information and evaluations to aid the decision making process, particularly when UW students need to balance their demanding study schedule and stressful course registration process.

Identify Information Technology Problem

Firstly, one problem is limited course information availability. Since UW officials should not provide subjective evaluations of courses, students lack access to comprehensive course information, student feedback, and detailed examination/assignment details on the existing platform. These are essential factors that students need to consider when deciding whether to enroll in a class or not. Secondly, there are few unofficial channels available and the information provided lacks timeliness and accuracy, resulting in limited reference significance. Manually searching for this information across various sources like Reddit is inefficient and unreliable. Moreover, there's no guarantee of accuracy, and the information might be outdated due to changes in curriculum and course staff. The third one is risk management and academic performance. The current system does not seem to adequately support students in managing the risks associated with enrolling in courses that may be too challenging or misaligned with their academic plans. This can adversely affect their GPA and overall academic progress. Therefore, we require a tool that offers accurate and comprehensive information to help make rational decisions.

Identify Existing Attempts

MyPlan Course Description

The MyPlan website is the main platform we use to select courses, and there is some course descriptions available when we are browsing the courses. While it provides some course descriptions and prerequisite information, it lacks essential details such as course structure, syllabus, policies, and grading criteria. This gap makes it difficult for students to gain a comprehensive understanding of the course before enrollment.

DawgPath

The main functionality of the DawgPath website is to include the course grade distribution over the past 5 years. This information can provide some insights into how difficult the class is, but the insight is very limited. High or low grade points cannot show the value of the course, also different teachers have different grading habits, resulting in variations in scores. It should not be the determining aspect when choosing courses.

RateMyProfessor

RateMyProfessor is an unofficial website where anyone can post reviews for professors according to their teaching quality and learning outcomes. However, since the website is unofficial, the reviews might not be reliable. Also, students might give professors a very low rating because they didn't get a good grade, so the reviews are highly biased. Moreover, the website primarily focuses on professor evaluations rather than course evaluations, making it challenging for students to assess course content accurately.

App Description

Target Users and Impact on Minority Groups

The primary users of this application will be University of Washington students who need to balance their demanding study schedule and stressful course registration process. They will be able to ultilize our website for making informed decision on course selection, ensuring that the courses they choose align with their academic goals and aspirations. The diverse user base our website aims to serve include:

  • Undergraduate students across all majors and years.
  • Graduate students who wish to explore courses outside their core curriculum.
  • Prospective students looking to understand the academic offerings and culture at UW.
  • Academic advisors and faculty members who assist students in their course planning.

The application is designed with inclusivity in mind, recognizing the varying needs and backgrounds of the UW student body. Special attention will be given to ensure the platform is accessible and provides equal opportunity for all students to share and access course-related information. Here are the potential positive impacts on minority groups:

  • The app will be designed to accommodate students with disabilities, ensuring compliance with accessibility standards.
  • The app's anonymous feedback mechanism allows for an honest exchange of experiences and concerns that can highlight areas of improvement for a more equitable learning environment.
  • For transfer and newly admitted students, accessing information about course and professor ratings from the student community might be challenging. Our website aims to bridge this information gap, providing them with valuable insights to ease their transition into studying at UW.

Interactive Features

  • Browse and search for course evaluations, download course syllabus (If no copyright issues), and bookmark courses for future references.
  • Verify student information (Register with uw.edu emails) (If no privacy or legal issues) and write course anonymous evaluations, upload course syllabus (If no copyright issues), and post or answer questions in the Q&A section. Verify Professor information and upload course materials and answer questions in the Q&A section.
  • Q&A section where students can ask anonymous questions to other students who previously took this course, both students and professor can answer the questions.
  • Visual Degree Planner: A tool that lets students visually map out their course paths over their academic career at UW, including prerequisites and co-requisites, to ensure they meet their graduation requirements.

What kind of information will the user be viewing

  • Numerical Figures: Course Rating, GPA, Professor Rating, and other aspects that can evaluate a course.
  • Graphs: Course Grade Distribution, statistics about students taking the course (year of study, major), satisfaction distribution, etc.
  • Textual Information: students' evaluations, questions and answers in the Q&A section, etc.

How this app help to solve the problem

This app combined all the advantages of the existing solutions described above. We want true and reasonable feedback from verified students to help other students choose the most suitable course. And by recommending supporting courses to help them better plan their academic careers. We also encourage the students and professors to communicate and create a supportive and caring community. We provides sufficient and unbiased information to help students gain a whole picture of the course and assist them to make rational decisions.
